Here is a diminished chord

If we move the first note down one fret toward the nut we end up with a Major 7th chord.

If we move the second note down one fred we get another Major 7th chord

Moving the third note down has the same result.

And finally, moving the last note down we get yet another Major 7th chord.

Isn't that neat?!

Now, let's look back at that first Major 7th chord we got. If we move that chord down until one finger is off the fretboard, we end up with a familiar chord. That's the E7 chord!

We'll call this the E7 shape. Where is the E in all this? Why, it's on the open string, string #2. That's the root of the chord. So, for all chords of this shape, the root will be on the 2nd string and you'll play it with your index finger.
